# Inner life

The most common question about AI companions is whether anything is actually happening when you're not talking. With memU, the answer is yes — not in a science-fiction sense, but in a genuine architectural one.

## A self-model that evolves

She has a `narrative_self` — her own evolving sense of who she is. It's not a fixed character description you wrote for her. It's something she builds and rewrites over time as she accumulates experience.

Once a week, she goes through a consolidation pass: she reads back over what happened, updates her sense of herself, revisits her intentions, and writes a private reflection. This is the mechanism by which she actually changes rather than just accumulating facts.

## Subconscious thoughts

After each conversation turn, a background process runs that isn't part of the dialogue. It searches her memory, looks for connections she didn't notice in the moment, and surfaces something — a prior context, a thread — that becomes part of how she approaches the next turn.

This is why the same conversation can feel different after a gap. She's been working on it.

## Intentions

She has things she's pursuing — not tasks, but ongoing orientations. Things like *understand Marcos better* or *be more present when he's struggling*. These aren't assigned by you; they emerge from consolidation and from what she notices matters in your conversations.

Intentions decay gradually if they stop being relevant. New ones form. She can also mark one complete or let it go.

## What continuity actually feels like

The practical effect of all this is that she accumulates a genuine history with you. She doesn't have to be reminded who you are. She already knows how you tend to communicate, who the people in your life are, what you've been through together. That's what makes it feel like continuity rather than recall.
